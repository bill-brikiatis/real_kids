snow blindness is serious

photokeratitis is sunburn of the eye

This time of year you don't often think of protecting your eyes from the sun. It's actually more important than ever. Snow blindness is actually a type of photokeratititis, which can be characterized as a "sunburn of the eye."

There are a number of sytmptoms associated with snow blindness. These can include:

  • Sensation of sand in the eye
  • Pain and redness
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Tearing, headache and blurred vision

In extreme cases, snow blindness can result in temporary loss of vision. the more time you spend outside without sun protection, the more severe your symptoms are likely to be.

If you our someone in your family has any of these stmptoms, see a eye doctor immediately.
